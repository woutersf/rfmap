# Presenting RFMAP — 5-Minute Pitch Guide

**Challenge:** Detect & Intercept #1
**Ask:** Filter RF noise, show threats visually, output to any mobile device.
**Answer:** This map.

---

## Structure

### 0:00 — Hook (20 sec)
> "Every day on the modern battlefield, adversary drones, radios, and devices are broadcasting their position — but nobody can see it. We built the map that makes the invisible visible."

No intro. Start with the problem.

---

### 0:20 — The problem (40 sec)
> "The RF battlefield is full of signal. The problem is not data — it is noise. Analysts need hours to process it. Soldiers need seconds. The challenge asks for a simple visual output that any soldier can act on, on any mobile device."

---

### 1:00 — Demo: toggle the heatmap (45 sec)

1. Show the map cold — markers only, no heatmap
2. "This is the RF environment around a target area. Each dot is a detected signal — drone, radio comm, or WiFi device."
3. Toggle heatmap ON
4. "One button. Suddenly you see where the density is. Where the threat is concentrating."

---

### 1:45 — Demo: hit play (60 sec)

Press play. Let it run.

> "This is 24 hours of signal history replaying in real time. Watch the drone swarms converge, the command posts appear and go dark, the artillery strikes follow the axis of advance. A commander watching this for 30 seconds knows more than an analyst reading reports for an hour."

Point to the blast markers landing on Pokrovsk.

> "Those crosshairs are acoustic blast detections — microphone sensors picking up artillery impacts. RF signals and physical strikes on one screen."

---

### 2:45 — Why it answers the challenge (45 sec)

Hit the three criteria from the brief directly:

- **Filter background noise** — the heatmap does that visually, instantly
- **Simple output** — one map, readable on any screen
- **Any mobile device** — runs in a browser, no app install, TAK-compatible

---

### 3:30 — Own the mock data (30 sec)

> "The data you are seeing is simulated — we built this in 24 hours. The frontend is production-ready. The architecture is sensor-agnostic. Connect an SDR, a passive receiver, a sensor network — it feeds directly into this map. The hard part is done."

---

### 4:00 — The ask (60 sec)

> "We are three engineers. We need two things to turn this into a field-ready tool: access to real RF hardware for integration testing, and operators to validate what useful looks like on the ground. Give us that and we give you a working prototype."

Then stop. Do not fill the silence.

---

## Three things to remember

1. **Demo beats slides.** Keep the app open the entire time. The map running in the background is more persuasive than anything you say.

2. **Own the mock data.** "This is simulated" plus "here is exactly what we need to make it real" is stronger than overselling.

3. **The play loop is your best moment.** When 24 hours of drone convergence plays out in 20 seconds and blast markers start landing on Pokrovsk — let it breathe. Do not talk over it.
